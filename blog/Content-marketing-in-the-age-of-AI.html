Content Marketing in the Age of AI

Is content marketing still relevant in the age of AI? The answer, without a doubt, is yes.

A lot has been said about how LLMs will bring in a new age for search. Although there is no question that they are very powerful tools, just as there is no doubt that they have very big issues, it is also true that many claims tend to be exaggerated and totally blown out of proportion.

Yes, the ways users seek information will most likely change as a result of AI. Yes, many people are already using AI tools to replace traditional searching mechanisms. However, it is not the case that this change has occurred in the way that they want you to believe. There is still a long way to go for this to happen.

My guess is that, while AI’s power and its use cases will keep growing, it will not replace simple things like looking for something specific on Google. No matter how powerful it becomes, it will not overthrow communities like Reddit where real people get to interact with one another. More importantly, it will not take the place of experts. AI is just not intended to become a one-size-fits-all tool that can do everything.

There is one thing to watch out for, though. AI tools like LLMs will definitely make it harder to make really good content. AI slop is real, and if you don’t watch out, it can become very easy to fall into its trap. However, this also poses an opportunity. The best content, that which is made with real insights of what users want, with a deep understanding of your organization and its audience, that’s the type of content that will stand out.

While the AI industry tries to convince you otherwise, you can continue pushing your content marketing.
