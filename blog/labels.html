Labels

Adding a label to something is a common thing to do. People everywhere do it, probably because it helps us structure our thoughts and the way we communicate. Expressing ideas without using labels would be very hard. For example, it would be very hard to express how one feels if there weren’t labels like sad or happy to refer to certain emotional states.

The same is valid for material things. We have a label for a thing we know as a fork and also for another we identify as a knife or a spoon. We even have a label for when these items are referred to in a collective and abstracted form. We call them cutlery. Part of the beauty of labels is precisely this. We can label things based on specificity, or on some other general norms.

This can be very useful, as it enriches our understanding and ways of expressing. However, it can also become a problem. What happens when we go too deep into the specifics of something? Think, for example, of the jargon that medical specialists use. It is no coincidence that normal people often find themselves confused by medical terms. They are too specific.

Something similar occurs in the other direction. When we abstract something too much, we also risk being misunderstood. An example here would be that of a philosopher using metaphysical terms to describe something that only exists as the subject of thought. For example, think of an orthogonal movement in the Deleuzian sense. Like in the previous example, the philosopher risks creating some sort of confusion, even if the paths are not the same.

Labels can also end up creating the opposite of what we are referring to. In the case of cutlery, using this label means that there must be other things that are not cutlery. Whare are those things exactly? Are they all easily identifiable as such? Sometimes these are not easy questions for which one can give a straightforward answer. Yet, labels tend to work, at least most of the time.

All of this is not to say that we should not use labels, but rather to acknowledge their use and, as a friendly reminder, avoid taking them for granted. Labels have the power to enclose things, physical or metaphysical, within certain boundaries. This helps us express ourselves. However, they can also end up having unintended consequences.
