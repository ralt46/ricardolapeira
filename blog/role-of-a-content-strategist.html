The Role of a Content Strategist

Most pepole believe the role of a Content Strategist requires working exclusively with SEO. Although this is an important part of the job in many companies, it is far from being the only thing a Content Strategist does, even when working in marketing.

From my personal experience, I have come to believe that what a Content Strategist actually does is work with information. This, of course, includes different forms of information. From working with written content like blog posts, or social media captions, to more advanced topics like UX Writing. I even take it as far as working on specialized documents like whitepapers, knowledge reports, and foresight exercises. Even working with information in the form of quantitative data like databases fits the Content Strategist’s role.

What ultimately brings all these things together is, in the first place, finding the right audience, understanding it, and then fine-tuning the message to communicate an idea clearly, sometimes pursuing a specific goal like attaining a behavior change or improving a business metric. This is usually referred to as a conversion metric, but that is a very general and boring term that does not really encompass what content does. Instead, I prefer to think of the goal in terms of outcomes, or in other words, what is it that you want to achieve?

The second thing that brings together all the different things a Content Strategist does is acting as a bridge. What I mean by that is that, in order to make something that stands out and achieves specific outcomes, content must be able to integrate the work of different people. A Content Strategist does not work in isolation. On the contrary, he depends on others to make things that are worth users’ time and attention.

Doing so is not so straight-forward, and that is why research also plays an important part of the Content Strategist’s role. Performing good research is, in my opinion, what differentiates great Content Strategists from average ones. Good research helps understand who you are talking to, what their frustrations are, how they think, how you can help them achieve what they want, and most importantly, it gives you evidence to make decisions. Instead of just acting on gut feelings, it provides a way to convince others about the right path forward.

So yes, a Content Strategist does much more than just optimize blog articles.
